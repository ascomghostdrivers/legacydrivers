The following information is from a post by Hugo Birch on the ASCOM Talk forum: https://ascomtalk.groups.io/g/Help/topic/118909137 

I had used the 32 bit FTDI USB drivers provided by the Diffraction Limited Staff (http://ftdichip.com/Drivers/D2XX.htm)
Alongside the MaxIMDLObsoleteDrivers I was able to get the camera working in MaxIM DL V6.

The following two files must be in the Maxim DL V5 folder, copy them from the Maxim DL v6 folder: DLUtilities.dllV5 and FTD2XX.dll
 
That was enough to get the camera working in Nebulosity, I could then use the Nebulosity ASCOM Driver to select the device in NINA (I downloaded the Nebulosity ASCOM driver from here: Nebulosity Ascom Driver · Issue #3 · celstark/OpenNebulosity
 
Then I wanted to connect the camera to NINA, but unfortunately, it was returning an Unable to connect error, reason: SensorType.
 
To fix this, I used dnSpy to edit C:\Program Files (x86)\Common Files\ASCOM\Camera\ASCOM.Nebulosity.Camera.dll this file, specifically iCameraV2 > SensorType which is showing PropertyNotImplementedException("SensorType", false). Edit the IL for get_SensorType(), and replace the body with just ldc.i4.2 and ret, which makes the driver return ASCOM sensor type 2 (RGGB) instead of throwing.

After clicking OK in dnSpy, use Save Module to write the patched DLL to disk, then replace the original DLL with the patched one after making a backup. Once that was done, NINA connected successfully through the Nebulosity ASCOM driver with no more SensorType error!
